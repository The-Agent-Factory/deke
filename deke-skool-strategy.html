01 · Overview

Executive summary

Deke Sharon is the most recognized authority in contemporary a cappella. Today that authority is monetized almost entirely through high-touch, non-recurring work. A Skool community converts the existing 25,000+ audience into predictable monthly recurring revenue — and a content asset that keeps working between live sessions.

The credentials are unmatched: Pitch Perfect, NBC's The Sing-Off, Broadway's In Transit, 2,500+ arrangements, four books, and a #1 Billboard credit. But the current revenue model — custom arrangements ($500–$5k), coaching ($200–$500/hr), and workshops ($5k–$25k+) — is lucrative yet doesn't compound, and it's capped by Deke's calendar.

This plan uses a three-tier membership — a low-priced on-ramp, a mid-priced growth tier, and a capped VIP cohort — to reach $25,000/month while concentrating Deke's live time (8+ hours/month) where it creates the most value. The headline: roughly 2% of a 25k audience converting across the three tiers gets there.

02 · Rationale

Why Skool, and why a community

  • Recurring beats one-off. One $99 arrangement is a transaction; one $99/month member is an asset worth ~$1,200/year and growing.
  • The content compounds. Every live class becomes a library lesson, then a social clip that recruits the next member. The work is done once and sells repeatedly.
  • Community is a moat. Peer feedback, challenges, and belonging drive retention in ways a course never can — members stay for each other, not just the teacher.
  • Skool is purpose-built. It bundles community feed, a course "classroom," events calendar, and gamification (leaderboards, levels) in one simple platform members already trust.

One platform reality to design around

A single Skool group has one price. So the three tiers are delivered as: (1) a free group as top of funnel, (2) one core paid group where Ensemble/Soloists content lives (higher-cadence Soloists material gated in the classroom), and (3) a capped VIP cohort sold as an add-on/upgrade. This is the standard, proven creator structure — and exactly what the planner models.

03 · Market

Audience & positioning

Positioning: Learn contemporary a cappella from the person who invented the modern sound.

Core segments — all already in Deke's orbit, and none with a single home to learn from him on an ongoing basis:

  • Collegiate & high-school singers and their music directors — the largest, most engaged group.
  • Community and semi-pro group members and organizers.
  • Choral educators and arrangers who want to modernize their craft.
  • Hobbyist arrangers and beatboxers chasing a specific skill.

The community becomes that home — and the natural top of the funnel for Deke's premium services.

06 · Economics

Monetization & the path to $25,000/month

  • Pricing rationale. $29 is an easy yes for a student; $99 is the "I'm serious" tier; $497 is genuinely premium but a fraction of Deke's $200–500/hr coaching — and capped.
  • VIP is the margin driver. Just 8 VIP members ≈ 280 Ensemble members in revenue. Filling and retaining the VIP cohort is the single highest-leverage activity.
  • Annual plans (offer ~2 months free) bring cash up front and cut churn — model these as a share of each tier once live.
  • Backend upsells. The community feeds Deke's existing high-ticket services — custom arrangements, workshops, university residencies — at member-only rates. It can increase the legacy business, not cannibalize it.
  • Referrals. Skool's structure makes member-get-member promotions easy; reward with free months.

Fees to net out

Budget ~12% blended for the Skool platform fee and Stripe processing when projecting take-home. At the target mix, net ≈ $22,400/month.

07 · Go-to-market

Launch plan

Phase 1 — Pre-launch (Weeks 1–3): build quietly

  • Stand up the Skool group(s), brand them, write the classroom outline.
  • Record the 2–3 flagship courses.
  • Open the free group and a founding-member waitlist; warm the email list and socials with teasers and guest-interview trailers.

Phase 2 — Launch (Week 4): go loud

  • Founding-member promo to the 25k+ list (limited-time price or bonus, real deadline).
  • A free live kickoff event that converts attendees into paid members on the call.
  • Open a scarce VIP cohort with a hard cap — this drives urgency.

Phase 3 — Scale (Months 2–3+): turn the flywheel

  • Publish on a steady cadence; repurpose every live into library + clips.
  • Run the guest series to keep filling the free group.
  • Optimize free→paid and paid→up-tier conversion; introduce annual plans and referrals.

Deke's job is to teach, show up live, and review VIP work — the parts only he can do.
