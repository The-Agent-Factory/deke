## 1. Executive summary

Deke Sharon is the most recognized authority in contemporary a cappella — Pitch Perfect, NBC's
The Sing-Off, Broadway's *In Transit*, 2,500+ arrangements, four books, and a #1 Billboard
credit. Today that authority is monetized almost entirely through **high-touch, non-recurring
work**: custom arrangements ($500–$5k), coaching ($200–$500/hr), and workshops ($5k–$25k+).
It's lucrative but it doesn't compound, and it's capped by Deke's calendar.

A **Skool community** converts the existing 25,000+ audience into **predictable monthly
recurring revenue (MRR)** and builds a content asset that keeps working between live sessions.
The plan below uses a **three-tier model** — a low-priced on-ramp, a mid-priced growth tier,
and a capped VIP cohort — to reach **$25,000/month** while concentrating Deke's live time
(8+ hours/month) where it creates the most value.

**The headline math:** ~2% of a 25k audience converting across the three tiers gets there.

## 2. Why Skool, and why a community

- **Recurring beats one-off.** One $99 arrangement is a transaction; one $99/month member is
  an asset worth ~$1,200/year and growing.
- **The content compounds.** Every live class becomes a library lesson, then a social clip
  that recruits the next member. The work is done once and sells repeatedly.
- **Community is a moat.** Peer feedback, challenges, and belonging drive retention in ways a
  course never can — members stay for each other, not just the teacher.
- **Skool is purpose-built.** It bundles community feed, a course "classroom," events calendar,
  and gamification (leaderboards, levels) in one simple platform that members already trust.

**One platform reality to design around:** a single Skool group has **one price**. So the three
tiers are delivered as:

1. **A free group** — top of funnel (the audience lands here).
2. **One core paid group** — the Ensemble/Soloists experience, with Soloists content gated
   inside the classroom and the higher cadence delivered there.
3. **A VIP cohort** — sold as an add-on (its own small group or an upgrade), capped to protect
   Deke's time and keep it premium.

This is the standard, proven creator structure — and it's exactly what the interactive planner
models.

## 3. Audience & positioning

**Positioning:** *Learn contemporary a cappella from the person who invented the modern sound.*

Core segments (all already in Deke's orbit):

- **Collegiate & high-school singers** and their **music directors** — the largest, most
  engaged group.
- **Community and semi-pro group** members and organizers.
- **Choral educators and arrangers** who want to modernize their craft.
- **Hobbyist arrangers and beatboxers** chasing a skill.

These people currently have no single home to learn directly from Deke on an ongoing basis.
The community becomes that home.

## 5. Content plan & cadence

**Content pillars:** Arranging · Vocal technique (blend, tuning, tone) · Vocal percussion ·
Performance & stage presence · Director/rehearsal craft · Career & business of a cappella ·
Stories from the industry (Pitch Perfect, Sing-Off, Broadway).

**Mapping Deke's 8+ live hours/month:**

| Live commitment | Tier reached | Approx. time |
|---|---|---|
| Weekly live class (4×/mo) | Ensemble + Soloists | ~4 hrs |
| Monthly group coaching | Soloists | ~1.5 hrs |
| VIP small-group call | VIP | ~1.5 hrs |
| VIP personal reviews (batched) | VIP | ~1–2 hrs |

Every live session is **recorded once and reused three ways**: (1) added to the course library,
(2) cut into short social clips for top-of-funnel growth, (3) summarized into the newsletter.

**Interview/guest series** is the growth flywheel — recurring conversations with Pitch
Perfect/Sing-Off alumni, working artists, collegiate directors, and industry producers. Guests
bring their audiences; clips travel; the free group fills.

**Launch the classroom with 2–3 flagship courses** (e.g. *Arranging in 10 Steps*, *Blend &
Tuning*, *Performance & Stage Presence*) and add one new module per month so there's always a
reason to stay subscribed.

## 6. Monetization & the path to $25,000/month

- **Pricing rationale:** $29 is an easy yes for a student; $99 is the "I'm serious" tier; $497
  is genuinely premium but a fraction of Deke's $200–500/hr coaching — and capped.
- **VIP is the margin driver.** Just 8 VIP members ≈ 280 Ensemble members in revenue. Filling
  and retaining the VIP cohort is the single highest-leverage activity.
- **Annual plans** (offer ~2 months free) bring cash up front and cut churn — model these as a
  share of each tier once live.
- **Backend upsells:** the community feeds Deke's existing high-ticket services — custom
  arrangements, workshops, university residencies — at member-only rates. The community can
  *increase* the legacy business, not cannibalize it.
- **Referrals:** Skool's structure makes member-get-member promotions easy; reward with free
  months.

**Fees to net out:** budget ~12% blended for the Skool platform fee and Stripe processing when
projecting take-home (the planner shows a "net after fees" figure).

See `deke-skool-planner.html` to stress-test any pricing/membership mix against the $25k goal.

## 7. Launch plan

**Phase 1 — Pre-launch (Weeks 1–3): build quietly.**
- Stand up the Skool group(s), brand them, write the classroom outline.
- Record the 2–3 flagship courses.
- Open the **free group** and a **founding-member waitlist**; start warming the email list and
  socials with teaser content and guest-interview trailers.

**Phase 2 — Launch (Week 4): go loud.**
- Founding-member promo to the 25k+ list (limited-time price or bonus, real deadline).
- A free **live kickoff event** that converts attendees into paid members on the call.
- Open a **scarce VIP cohort** with a hard cap — this drives urgency.

**Phase 3 — Scale (Months 2–3+): turn the flywheel.**
- Publish on a steady cadence; repurpose every live into library + clips.
- Run the guest series to keep filling the free group.
- Optimize the free→paid and paid→up-tier conversion; introduce annual plans and referrals.

## 8. Operations & metrics

Day-to-day the community is run by the brand's **communications & operations manager**:
content scheduling and production support, member onboarding, moderation, the guest-booking
pipeline, email/social repurposing, and reporting. Deke's job is to **teach, show up live, and
review VIP work** — the parts only he can do.

**Setup:** Skool group(s) + Stripe; email platform integration; a simple onboarding sequence;
clear community guidelines.

**Track weekly:** MRR · churn rate · conversion (free→paid, paid→up-tier) · VIP cohort fill ·
member LTV. These tie naturally into the automation ideas already documented for the brand
(see `docs/Deke_Sharon_AI_Agent_Ecosystem.md`) — e.g. concierge onboarding and content
repurposing agents.

## 9. Risks & mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Deke's time gets stretched | Cap VIP; batch reviews; reuse recordings; ops manager absorbs everything non-teaching. |

| Skool single-price limit | Free funnel + core paid group + VIP add-on cohort (Section 2). |
| Slow start | Founding-member promo + live launch event + waitlist scarcity. |
